Research Profile

Operations & Economics scholar studying how screening/gatekeeping, allocation, and pricing shape behavior and the information managers rely on. I build game-theoretic models and experiments to design implementable mechanisms for hiring and service systems, organ/platform allocation, and data-driven screening. Published in Management Science, Theoretical Economics, Economic Theory, GEB, with ML-incentives work in ICML and NeurIPS; supported by ISF (personal & equipment) and MOST grants.

Job talk: The Gatekeeper Effect — OM mechanisms for screening under uncertainty (Management Science, 2026).

Selected Pipeline (R&R / Major Revision)

  • Theorist Toolbox: Tools for Agent-Based LLM-Assisted Economic Theory Research
    — working paper; arXiv:2606.22337 (2026).
    Contribution: a verification-first protocol for doing economic theory with large language models, instantiated as three methods that differ in how the work is checked (a single disciplined pass; an adversarial prover–verifier pair; a structured multi-agent project with a reviewer gate); argues that external verification, not model capability, is the binding design variable.

  • Counterbalancing Learning and Strategic Incentives in Allocation Markets
    — Manufacturing & Service Operations Management, major revision.
    Contribution: designs mechanisms that balance information acquisition and strategic incentives in dynamic allocation (e.g., organ/platform markets).

  • Sequential Fundraising and Mutual Insurance
    — JPE Micro (Journal of Political Economy: Microeconomics), revise & resubmit.
    Contribution: characterizes when sequential fundraising with risk-sharing generates inefficiency and provides implementable design fixes; delivers testable comparative statics.

  • Classification Under Strategic Self-Selection
    — ICML 2024; journal extension in preparation, target: Management Science / Information Systems Research.
    Contribution: entry-aware classification under endogenous applicant pools; screening rules robust to self-selection.

  • A Graph-Based Mechanism for Assigning Displaced Individuals (with Y. Doron & A. Kulik)
    — working paper in preparation; targets: Management Science / Operations Research (algorithms & simulations), Economic Theory / GEB (theory framework), ACM EC / WINE (tractable subclasses).
    Contribution: introduces a graph-theoretic resettlement mechanism that partitions a weighted social network into capacity-constrained groups while minimizing severed social ties; proves the cut-minimizing assignment is stable and develops a scalable cycle-based heuristic with strong empirical performance.

  • Placement Games with Threshold-Linear Congestion: A Graphical Potential-Game Approach to Decentralized Assignment (with S. Horowitz & Y. Doron)
    — working paper in preparation; targets: Management Science / Operations Research, Games and Economic Behavior.
    Contribution: models decentralized assignment of agents to localities over a weighted social network as an exact potential game with threshold-linear congestion; establishes existence and convergence of pure Nash equilibria and tight price-of-anarchy bounds for socially-aware resettlement. Extends S. Horowitz's MSc thesis.

  • Donor-Hospital Concentration and Acceptance Behavior After KAS250 in Kidney Allocation (with S. Tzidkolov)
    — working paper in preparation; targets: M&SOM, Health Care Management Science.
    Contribution: center-level before/after study of the 2021 KAS250 U.S. kidney-allocation reform; measures how donor-hospital concentration (normalized HHI) shifted and links within-center diversification to declines in organ acceptance, using OPTN STAR offer data.

  • Batch Offering Under Uncertainty: A Common-Value Stopping Game Model for Organ Allocation (with V. Bar Nir & Y. Cornfeld)
    — working paper; presented at ORSIS 2026 (Technion, May 12–13, 2026); targets: Management Science / Operations Research, Games and Economic Behavior.
    Contribution: casts batch offering in organ allocation as a common-value Dynkin stopping game; proves existence of a symmetric threshold equilibrium, characterizes the asymptotic barrier strategy, and shows numerically that signal accuracy dominates batch size as a welfare lever — the success of batch offering depends less on how many centers compete and more on how good their information is.

  • Build the Player: Teaching Game Theory by Designing Strategic Agents
    — INFORMS Transactions on Education, submitted.
    Contribution: a pedagogical approach that teaches game theory by having students design and build strategic agents, turning equilibrium concepts into hands-on engineering of the players themselves.

  • Fairness-Constrained Actor–Critic for Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (with O. Sheetrit & S. Shperberg)
    — NeurIPS, submitted (under review).
    Contribution: develops a fairness-constrained actor–critic method for multi-agent reinforcement learning; Koren leads the economic-theory aspects of the work.
