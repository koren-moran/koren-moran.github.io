Research Profile

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Industrial Engineering and Management at Ben-Gurion University of the Negev, where I head the Game Theory and Agentic Design Lab.

My research program is built at the intersection of microeconomic theory, operations, and information/data science. I use the rigorous tools of game theory and mechanism design to address concrete operational challenges in markets and platforms. My work develops and analyzes models that incorporate information frictions, learning, and strategic incentives to design more efficient and robust systems. This interdisciplinary approach builds upon my foundation in theoretical economics to address the applied, problem-driven challenges central to modern operations and data-driven market design.

The core goal of my lab is to create a research engine where fundamental theory is developed and rigorously tested through experiments and computational methods, using synthetic or real-world data, to generate practical insights. We tackle multifaceted problems in areas like hiring and screening processes, organ allocation, and online platforms. This philosophy extends to my teaching, where I apply the same principles of mechanism and system design to create innovative and resilient pedagogical solutions.

The interdisciplinary nature of my research is reflected in my publications, which appear in leading journals across economics (Theoretical Economics, Games and Economic Behavior), operations (Management Science), and top-tier computer science and machine learning conferences (NeurIPS,ACM EC ICML).

My research has been supported by several prestigious awards, including a personal grant from the Israel Science Foundation (ISF), the Shamir Fellowship for returning scientists, and the Fulbright Postdoctoral Fellowship.

Prior to joining BGU, I held postdoctoral fellowships at Tel Aviv University, Harvard University, and Stanford University. I hold a Ph.D. in Economics from the Technion.
